# Reality claims need contact with current conditions

## Observation

Strategic conversations become more useful when claims about the future are repeatedly tested against present constraints, capabilities, incentives, and operating habits.

## Interpretation

This supports the working idea that aspiration is useful only when it is connected to current reality. The risk is treating vision language as progress before the organisation has understood the conditions it must move from.

## Evidence Needed

- Examples from strategy engagements where naming current constraints changed the plan.
- Website articles or field notes describing the gap between stated ambition and operating reality.
- Counterexamples where aspiration created useful momentum before current reality was fully mapped.
