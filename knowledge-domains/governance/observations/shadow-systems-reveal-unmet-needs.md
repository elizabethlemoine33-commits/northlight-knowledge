# Shadow systems reveal unmet needs

## Observation

Unofficial spreadsheets, side channels, and local processes often appear where formal systems do not answer the practical needs of the people doing the work.

## Interpretation

Shadow systems should be investigated before they are removed. They may reveal missing functionality, unclear ownership, slow approvals, or information needs that the formal system has not acknowledged.

## Evidence Needed

- Examples of shadow systems that performed a legitimate operational function.
- Cases where removing a workaround without replacing its function made the system worse.
- Counterexamples where shadow systems primarily created avoidable risk.
