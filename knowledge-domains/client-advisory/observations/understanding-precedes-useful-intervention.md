# Understanding precedes useful intervention

## Observation

Recommendations become more durable when they are delayed until the advisor understands the conditions that created the request, not only the request itself.

## Interpretation

The first stated problem may be accurate, partial, or a proxy for a deeper issue. Useful intervention depends on distinguishing those cases before prescribing action.

## Evidence Needed

- Examples where the initial request changed after discovery.
- Examples where acting on the initial request solved the wrong problem.
- Counterexamples where the requested intervention was exactly what was needed.
