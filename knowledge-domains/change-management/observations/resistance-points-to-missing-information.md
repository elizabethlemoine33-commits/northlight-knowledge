# Resistance points to missing information

## Observation

Resistance often becomes easier to interpret when it is treated as information about uncertainty, risk, identity, workload, or past experience rather than as opposition to change itself.

## Interpretation

This observation does not mean all resistance is valid or actionable. It means resistance can contain diagnostic value before it becomes a management problem.

## Evidence Needed

- Examples where resistance identified a real implementation risk.
- Examples where resistance reflected unresolved history rather than the proposed change.
- Counterexamples where resistance persisted after legitimate concerns were addressed.
