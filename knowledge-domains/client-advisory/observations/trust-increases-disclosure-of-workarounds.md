# Trust increases disclosure of workarounds

## Observation

People are more likely to describe informal workarounds, failed handoffs, and local adaptations after they believe the conversation is meant to understand the work rather than assign blame.

## Interpretation

Trust does not create reality, but it changes how much reality becomes visible. Advisory work that skips trust-building may mistake a partial account for the whole system.

## Evidence Needed

- Field notes from interviews where informal practices emerged later in the conversation.
- Examples of workarounds that were absent from formal process documentation.
- Counterexamples where formal records were more accurate than interview accounts.
