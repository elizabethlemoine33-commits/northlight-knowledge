# Learning requires selective retention

## Observation

Organisations often benefit from broad learning inputs, but their knowledge systems become more useful when only durable insights, decisions, patterns, and evidence are intentionally retained.

## Interpretation

Learning freely does not mean keeping everything. Retention has a cost, and unfiltered accumulation can make future retrieval and interpretation harder.

## Evidence Needed

- Examples where too much retained material made knowledge harder to use.
- Examples where lightweight filtering improved retrieval or reuse.
- Counterexamples where raw archives were necessary for later analysis.
