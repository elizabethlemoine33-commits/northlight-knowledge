# Frameworks

Frameworks synthesize repeated observations, evidence, current understandings, and principles into reusable models.

One framework per file.

## What a Framework Is For

A framework is a starting position, not a container reality has to fit.

Think of sorting a garage. You don't know in advance exactly how many boxes you'll need or what every label should say. You do a rough sort first — most of what you own goes into a handful of boxes you set up ahead of time, because most of it is common enough to belong somewhere obvious. What's left over is the mess: the stuff that doesn't fit cleanly. That's where the real thinking happens.

Frameworks work the same way. They hold what you already know, so your attention is free for what you don't. They tell you where most of what you'll find on a new engagement probably belongs, so you can move fast on the common ground and spend your effort on what's unique to this situation.

The moment reality contradicts the framework, the framework loses. Not the reality.
