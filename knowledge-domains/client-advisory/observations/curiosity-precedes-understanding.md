# Curiosity precedes understanding

## Observation

When advisory conversations begin with genuine curiosity, clients tend to share more context than when the conversation begins with assessment, diagnosis, or recommendation.

## Interpretation

Curiosity appears to create the conditions for better understanding. It slows premature interpretation and makes room for details that would otherwise remain invisible.

## Evidence Needed

- Examples from discovery calls where initial assumptions changed after open inquiry.
- Notes from client interviews where curiosity surfaced constraints not present in the original request.
- Counterexamples where a direct diagnostic posture was more appropriate.
