# Repository Method

> *How knowledge is created, refined, and preserved within Northlight.*

---

## Purpose

This repository exists to capture the evolution of thinking.

It is intentionally designed to distinguish between what has been observed, what is currently believed, and what has repeatedly proven to be true.

Not every idea becomes a principle.

Not every observation deserves a framework.

Knowledge develops through evidence.

---

# The Knowledge Lifecycle

Ideas move through the repository in a deliberate progression.

```
Practice
    ↓
Observation
    ↓
Evidence
    ↓
Current Understanding
    ↓
Core Principle
    ↓
Framework
    ↓
Application
```

Each stage has a different purpose.

---

# Practice

Everything begins in practice.

Practice includes:

- client engagements
- internal work
- conversations
- experiments
- successes
- failures
- research
- lived experience

Practice produces raw material.

Practice is never skipped.

---

# Observation

An observation records something that has been noticed.

Observations should avoid explanation.

They should describe what happened before suggesting why it happened.

Good observations are:

- specific
- descriptive
- repeatable
- free from unnecessary interpretation

Example:

> Executive meetings became shorter after decision ownership was clarified.

That is an observation.

---

# Evidence

Evidence strengthens observations.

Evidence may come from:

- multiple client engagements
- published research
- internal metrics
- interviews
- published articles
- historical records
- case studies

Evidence asks:

**"Have we seen this often enough to take it seriously?"**

Evidence does not prove certainty.

It increases confidence.

---

# Current Understanding

Current understandings are working explanations.

They represent our best interpretation of the evidence available today.

They remain open to revision.

Every current understanding should be treated as provisional.

Future evidence may strengthen it, modify it, or invalidate it.

---

# Core Principles

Core principles have demonstrated consistent value across multiple situations.

They represent ideas that have remained useful despite changing industries, organisations, technologies, and circumstances.

Core principles should change rarely.

When they do change, the evidence should be substantial.

---

# Frameworks

Frameworks are practical tools.

They translate principles into action.

Frameworks should answer questions like:

- How do we apply this?
- What sequence should we follow?
- What decisions need to be made?
- What should we measure?

Frameworks are not principles.

Multiple frameworks may emerge from a single principle.

---

# Application

Knowledge only becomes valuable when it improves real work.

Applications include:

- advisory engagements
- workshops
- software
- AI systems
- operating procedures
- presentations
- published writing

Application produces new observations.

The cycle begins again.

---

# Confidence

Every document should identify its current level of confidence.

Suggested levels:

**Low**

Limited evidence.
Requires further observation.

**Medium**

Repeated evidence.
Useful in many situations.

**High**

Consistently supported through repeated application.

Confidence describes the maturity of the idea.

It does not imply certainty.

---

# Document Status

Each substantive document should include:

```
Status:
Draft | Active | Mature | Archived

Confidence:
Low | Medium | High

Evidence:
(list supporting sources)

Last Reviewed:
YYYY-MM-DD
```

This encourages continuous learning rather than static documentation.

---

# Repository Principles

When contributing to this repository:

Observe before concluding.

Separate evidence from interpretation.

Record uncertainty honestly.

Revise when better evidence appears.

Prefer practical experience over abstract theory.

Preserve the evolution of ideas.

Avoid rewriting history.

---

# Why This Matters

Knowledge is often treated as a collection of answers.

Northlight treats knowledge as a process.

The value of this repository is not simply the conclusions it contains.

Its value lies in preserving how those conclusions were reached.
